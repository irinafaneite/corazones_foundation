Who We Are

We are a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization committed to the prevention and care of gender-based violence, offering education programs, psychological and emotional support.

At Corazones of Courage Foundation, we believe that education and support are fundamental to breaking the cycle of gender-based violence.

Our mission is to support women who have been victims of violence, helping them rebuild their lives with dignity, safety, and autonomy. We provide tools for women to regain their confidence, recognize themselves as survivors, and find new opportunities. Our goal is for them to live a life free from violence.

Our work not only seeks to assist women survivors but also to prevent violence by promoting education about healthy relationships and personal boundaries. The goal is for everyone to learn to recognize warning signs and what actions to take in the face of gender-based violence.

Our Programs

Hearts in Action

Prevention is the key to stopping violence before it happens. Hearts in Action is our program focused on educating communities about gender-based violence and how to recognize its signs.

Through workshops, talks, and awareness campaigns, we teach:

  • Warning signs to identify dangerous relationships and behaviors.
  • How to act if you witness a situation of violence.
  • How to support a person at risk, providing help without revictimizing them.
  • The concept of healthy love, based on respect, freedom, and autonomy.

We want more people to be able to identify, prevent, and act against violence, creating support networks that save lives.

Courage to Heal

For those who have experienced violence, healing is a necessary but challenging journey. Courage to Heal is our six-month support program for women survivors, where we provide comprehensive assistance to rebuild their safety and confidence.

This program includes:

  • Weekly classes: experiential spaces where we combine theory and experience to discuss emotion management, healing, self-confidence, healthy sexuality, boundaries, etc. A curriculum aimed at learning to love in a healthy way.
  • Group emotional support: in each meeting, participants speak freely without judgment about their experiences.
  • Individual psychoeducational sessions: each participant is assigned to a specialist psychologist who accompanies them throughout the program in an individual space to work on psychoeducation and processing what happened.
  • Comprehensive support in situations arising from violence: we support our participants in emergencies resulting from violence, such as legal proceedings, housing needs, employment, and partnerships with companies that can integrate them into workplaces.

We accompany women survivors to live a life free from violence in the future.
